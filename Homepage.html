Confidence Interval for Mean

Known Variance

X̄ ± Zα/2 × σ/√n
Explanation: Use this formula when population variance (σ²) is known and population is normal, or sample size is large (n ≥ 30). Uses standard normal Z distribution.

2

Confidence Interval for Mean

Unknown Variance - Large Sample | n ≥ 30

X̄ ± Zα/2 × S/√n
Explanation: When variance is unknown but sample size is large (n ≥ 30), use sample standard deviation (S) instead of σ. By Central Limit Theorem, we can still use Z distribution.
σ² unknown n ≥ 30 S (sample)

3

Confidence Interval for Mean

t-Student | Small Sample n < 30

X̄ ± t(α/2, n-1) × S/√n
Explanation: For small samples (n < 30) with unknown variance, use t-Student distribution instead of Z. This distribution depends on degrees of freedom (df = n-1) and accounts for uncertainty in variance estimation.
n < 30 t-Student df = n-1

4

Confidence Interval for Proportion

Population Proportion | P̂

P̂ ± Z × √[P̂(1-P̂)/n]
Explanation: For calculating confidence interval for population proportion (like voters proportion, satisfied customers). Uses normal approximation with condition nP̂ ≥ 5 and n(1-P̂) ≥ 5.
Binary data P̂ = X/n Normal approximation

5

Confidence Interval for Variance

Chi-Square Distribution | σ²

[(n-1)S²/χ²α/2] < σ² < [(n-1)S²/χ²1-α/2]
Explanation: For calculating confidence interval for population variance (σ²) or standard deviation (σ). Requires Chi-Square distribution and assumes normal population. Interval is asymmetric around S².
Chi-Square Asymmetric interval df = n-1

Sample Size Calculation

Sample Size Determination | n

n = (Z × σ / e)²
Explanation: To determine required sample size before conducting study to achieve certain confidence level with specified margin of error (e). Useful for research planning and determining budget and resources needed.
